Vermont Tech Community Tech Challenge

A Community-Run, Community-Focused Innovation Challenge for Students

The Prompt

Create a computer or mobile game that speaks to, addresses, brings awareness to, or otherwise is related to a challenge faced by your community.

Logistics

  • Teams register with adult leads (non-school-associated teams are welcome!).
  • Teams must consist of at least 3 people.
  • The team leads are given information packs regarding how the challenge will be run, and offering guidance.
  • There is a virtual orientation for team leads, introducing them to some tools and answering questions.
  • The challenge kicks-off on September 8th.
  • Teams must deliver their submission on the day of Tech Jam.
  • Attendees of the tech jam play the games and rank their favorites.
  • The winners are announced at the end of the tech jam and receive prizes.

Details

  • The organizing team will always be available to answer questions from the team leads.
  • There will be a Vermont Tech Community Discord available for all participants where they can ask questions, hang out, and work together.
  • Weekly coding/building sessions.
  • Organizing team and mentors will be available to offer 30-60 minute scheduled "lessons" to the teams on tech and building games.
  • Teams should include more than tech-people! Games need designers, identifying challenges requires civics-oriented people, games need story tellers, and games need testers!
  • All participants get free tickets to PyData.
